we gentrified our grandmother



Gloria tended her garden each day, pruning and planting flowers that never died, in a foreclosed
house on a dead end street. In the dark, camouflaged men walked the streets with flashlights.
The morning after, everything she owned was in the yard, memories of her memories, strewn
around like wreckage after Ivan. A groundskeeper’s family collected plants both living and dead,
packed furniture into the back of their truck and drove away. Are these where flea market goods
come from? A blonde woman carried a potted plant through three neighboring yards, scuttling
like a crab in cut-off jeans, to finally rest the bounty claimed in her own.

Gloria disappeared. She had had a stroke, soft teeth, and didn’t speak much. She lived alone
with no husband. She embodied the strength of the silent generation. When she left, she didn’t
come back. A dumpster came and collected pieces of an old woman’s legacy. They will probably
build condos.

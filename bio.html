ana zim

bio / portfolio / statement

Ana Zimitravich (sometimes Ana Zed) is a baker and fledgling artist from Atlanta, GA. As a baker, their craftsmanship ventures into historical perspectives of dishes borne of scarcity (and abundance by comparing political class struggles), gastronomic traditions, and sculpture in dough. Their perspective on art is literally an obsession with perspectives. As a strong proponent for mental health, they utilize gouache, pen and pixels to explore the endless search for objective "reality" or "truth;" borne of the competition between Rene Descartes' method of personal deduction ("I think therefore I am") and Plato's quest for a "universal truth," which is fabled to exist independently from the individual, and could only be seen by "God." Often in life individuals are caused to question what is "true" for them, by comparing what is "true" for others. Ana explores this existential struggle by painting their own reality, juxtaposing what is seemingly peaceful with what is deceptively anxious. Ana is applying to art school, with the hopes of expanding on this infatuation with fantastical perspectives of "truth," traversing it from fine art to the digital world through code, pixellation, and animation.
